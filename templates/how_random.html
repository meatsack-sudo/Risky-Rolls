It's Pretty Random

My dice rolls are calculated using Python's secrets library.

How does secrets work? And what is it?

secrets is a Python library that contains functions for generating random combinations of characters or numbers.

What makes secrets unique is its use of your system’s entropy pool, gathering randomness from unpredictable sources like hardware noise and timing quirks. This isn’t just "looks random" randomness — it’s as close to true randomness as a computer can get. Every roll is independent and unpredictable, even if someone somehow knew the last 1,000 rolls.

Why I use secrets (and not random)

Randomness is tricky. For this app, I rely on Python’s secrets library, which uses a cryptographically secure random number generator (CSPRNG). Unlike the random module, which works great for casual tasks, secrets generates randomness that’s truly unpredictable and secure. It’s the same method used for encryption and secure passwords — the real deal.

Why not just use random?

The random module is solid for quick simulations and games, but it’s not truly random. It uses a pseudo-random number generator (PRNG), meaning the results are predictable if someone knows the "seed." This works fine for many applications, but it’s not good enough for dice rolls where fairness matters. Imagine someone reverse-engineering the next roll — not cool.

That’s why I chose secrets. It ensures every roll is genuinely unpredictable and fair.

What does this mean for you?

  • Every roll is unpredictable and can’t be guessed.
  • No one (not even me!) knows the next roll.
  • If you’re losing, it’s not the system’s fault — you’re just having a bad day.

TL;DR

I use secrets for dice rolls because it’s more secure and less predictable than random. Your rolls are fair, secure, and truly random. If the dice aren’t in your favor, it’s not rigged — it’s just bad luck. Keep rolling, and your time will come!
