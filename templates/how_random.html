Why We Use secrets for Random Numbers

Hey there, dice rollers! We know that luck can be a fickle friend, and when you’re on a losing streak, it’s tempting to blame the "random number generator." But we’re here to set the record straight and give you a peek behind the curtain on how our app keeps it fair, unpredictable, and (most importantly) truly random.

Why We Use secrets (and not random)

For generating random numbers, we’re using a Python library called secrets. But what makes it special? Unlike the standard random module, secrets gets its random numbers from something called a cryptographically secure random number generator (CSPRNG). That’s a fancy way of saying it taps into system-level randomness — the kind that’s unpredictable, untraceable, and virtually impossible to guess. It’s the same kind of randomness used in things like encryption, secure passwords, and online security.

How Does It Work?

Instead of using an algorithm that "feels random," secrets pulls randomness directly from the system’s entropy pool. This pool collects randomness from unpredictable sources like hardware noise, timing differences, and other small quirks of the system. It’s basically as close to "true randomness" as you’re going to get on a computer. This means every dice roll is unpredictable, even if someone somehow knew the last 1,000 rolls.

Why Not Use random Instead?

Glad you asked! The random module is great for things like simulations, games, and quick randomness, but it’s not truly random. Instead, it uses something called a pseudo-random number generator (PRNG). It’s "random" in the sense that it’s hard to see the pattern with your eyes, but if someone knew the "seed" (the starting point), they could predict every "random" number that comes next. Yikes!

While random is totally fine for simple tasks, it’s not quite up to the challenge for **dice rolls that people take seriously**. Imagine a clever player figuring out the next roll in advance — not fun for anyone. That’s why we’re using secrets instead.

What Does This Mean for You?

  • Every dice roll is unpredictable and truly random.
  • Nobody (not even us!) can predict the next roll.
  • Blaming "the system" for bad luck? Sorry, but you might just be on a cold streak.

TL;DR

We use secrets to generate random numbers because it’s **more secure, more random, and less predictable** than the standard random module. Your dice rolls are safe from prediction, tampering, and manipulation. So, if the dice aren't in your favor, it’s not us — it’s just bad luck. Keep rolling, and your time will come!
